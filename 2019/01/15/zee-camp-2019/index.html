Zee Camp 2019

15 Jan 2019

A 14 day camp organized for transgender community.

Those forteen days were unforgettable. It was a totally new experience for me. That was the first time, we closely dealt with the transgender community. We (Pehia Foundation) tried our best to make them feel comfortable, to create welcoming environment for them. 13 days, 3 different courses, around 10‚Äì15 transgender persons from all over Kerala.

In this blog, I would like to share my personal experiences working with them and the curiosity I had while being with them.

The venue was Santhigiri Ashram in Aluva. Zee Camp 2019 was a collaborative effort of the Pehia Foundation & Marthoma Navodaya Society. The event was from Jan 5th, 2019 to Jan 13th, 2019.

The program agenda was like Advanced Microsoft Office training in the first three days, then Introduction to Programming using Python for the next three days and in the last three days an Introduction to Web Development.

Since Enfa (cofounder of Pehia Foundation) had a class and part-time internship, she visited the program after her work. Me and Diya Liza Varghese (Volunteer) was there at the venue full time. Thank you so much to the Marthoma Navodaya Society for supporting us on this journey. They arranged food and accommodation for us and as well as for participants and speakers.

Day 0

Jan 4th, 2019

I was there at Santhigiri Ashram by evening 5 pm. We were expecting our fellow participants to be there by 6 pm. They came late because some strike was announced for the next two days all over Kerala. We(Me and Enfa) ‚Äòwere so happy. Because it was our dream to do something useful for the transgender community. In the initial stages of the Pehia Foundation, there were so many people who discouraged us from not to involve in transgender communities. Actually, they were not saying it, it felt like a warning to us.

We were scared too. I agree. We were afraid of the unknown. But we didn‚Äôt give up. We find different ways to reach these communities and thanks to Pirate Praveen and Sooraj Kenoth for connecting with Vijaya Raja Malika and Jijo Kuriakose from Queerala. We used to go for monthly meetups of Queerala to know the community and its members.

Finally the effort we put in started showing results. Our participants are here. We had dinner together. We took the initiative to build small conversations with them and we made sure that they‚Äôre comfortable. From the first day itself, they started their life experiences with us. They shared their feelings when their family and society rejected them. Some of them have not yet revealed their identity in their family. We sat with them and listen to them. We came to the problems they faced that we‚Äôd never imagined were there.

Advanced Microsoft Office Training

On the first course, we only had four participants, and Mr. Saji Kumar was their tutor. Saji sir has 6-year experience in training MS office. When I searched for a trainer, first concerned was whether they will be comfortable in mentoring the transgender community. I was really scared by the societal stereotyping which we were trying to undo through Pehla Foundation. When I spoke to Saji sir, he said that he had never trained transgender people and he doesn‚Äôt know how it will go. This was his only concern. I felt like we came to the right person.

He really was. Three day MS Office training turned out to be the best of three sessions we have planned. Our participants was so happy and comfortable with their mentor.

‚ÄúSaji sir treated us like his small sister. ‚Äú- feedback from one of our Zee camp fellow.

In these three days, they covered MS Word, MS power point and MS Excel.

Too many people asked us ‚ÄúWhy MS Office training?‚Äù

My answer to the question is, we organized Zee Camp for sharing some knowledge with the transgender community and we realized what they need is skills to get employment. We were not trying to make them programmers. This MS training was to upskill the community members so that they found more employable in-office tasks.

Introduction to Programming using Python

The second session was the introduction to programming using python and Anusree S, team members of Pehia Foundation take lead for the session. An intro to git and GitHub were also included in the curriculum.

‚ÄúEngaging with them was a different experience in my life,‚Äù Anusree S said.

Our team was as curious as I was. This was our experiment event with the transgender community. Before the event, we were just quality advocated I would say. We tried to spread gender awareness through our programs. We held healthy discussions on gender awareness in every program we organize.

I do remember, I and Enfa went for a talk together and after our session, one girl from the audience met us in person after the session and said that she is a bisexual. That time we were not connected with the LGBTQI community like Queerala. If we were connected at least it would have helped her to get mental/emotional support from the Queerala community.

On the second day, we had Rahul Ramesh, CTO, Entri App for mentoring our Zee camp fellows. Some of them were really interested in learning programming and others find it very difficult to understand the logic.

On the third day, we were discussing the Transgender bill Amendment. You can find my views on Transgender Bill here soon.

Introduction to Website Development

Mr. Geon George was the trainer. He was an engineering student and a good friend of Diya, our Ze camp volunteer. Geon is a great person, a simple thank you is not enough to thank him for supporting us and for being there in Pehia‚Äôs dream project.

Our number of participants increased. For web development, we had ten participants. Both transmen and transwomen persons. I met Hrithik there. He is a very charm person and soon we become friends. He is working as News Reader at Jeevan TV now. He and Tripthi Shetty become the second transgender couple in Kerala.

Geon‚Äôs session was very interactive. He started it with the history of internet and web. I had the privilege to assist him with this track. While he explained how to use Visual Studio Code and HTML on the first day, I give them challenges to solve using what they have learned in the previous track. On the third day, Geon introduced varies web frameworks and resources to learn it. Because we know that with one or two days‚Äô session none can master a skill. They need to practice what they‚Äôve learned. So he shared some valuable resources with them.

Giri Chandanakadan, a transman and a Pehia Zee camp fellow 2019 have been placed in Profisglobal as a web/graphics designer. I hope Pehia will be able to host more such camps for transgender communities.

Zee camp 2019 was one of the dream projects of Pehla Foundation which we really work hard for it to happen. Our sincere thanks to Preeti, program coordinator from Marthoma Navodaya Society for making this event possible and Rev Binu for providing the venue, food, and accommodation.

I hope we can organize more impactful programs in future.
